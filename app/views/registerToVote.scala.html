Register to vote

Before you can vote in UK elections and referendums, you need to have your details added to the electoral register (sometimes called the ‘electoral roll’).

You can also use this service to update your details on the electoral register.

What you will need

You’ll need your National Insurance number (if you have one).

Before you start

Age and nationality

You need to be:

  • 16 or over
  • British, or a citizen of a Commonwealth or EU country

National Insurance number

You'll need to provide evidence of your identity. The easiest way is to enter your National Insurance number.

If you can't supply a National Insurance number, you'll need to post copies of identity documents to your local electoral register office once you've completed the application.

What you need to know

Takes around 10 minutes.

Other ways to apply

To apply by post, download the register to vote form [PDF, 128KB].

9Tell us if you want information about postal or proxy voting

You’ll get a polling card from your local council shortly before each election. The polling card will tell you which polling station to vote at.

You can also apply to vote by post or arrange for someone else to vote for you (called voting ‘by proxy’).

10Tell us whether to include your details on the open register

The open register is an edited version of the electoral register that’s available for sale to anyone (for example, companies or charities who want to send you information).

You can choose whether your details are included on the open register. It doesn’t affect your right to vote.

11Tell us how to contact you

Register to vote

Your application is finished

Your application will be sent to your local registration office. They will contact you - either to confirm that your details have been entered onto the electoral register, or to ask you for more information.

Once your details are on the register, you’ll be be sent information about elections that are coming up (including where to go to cast your vote).
